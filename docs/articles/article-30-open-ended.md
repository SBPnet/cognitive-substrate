# Stage 29: Open-Ended Intelligence

*This article accompanies Stage 29 of the cognitive-substrate project. It describes open-ended evolution mode: continuous capability search under constitutional constraints and developmental readiness gates.*

## Beyond fixed capability

Most agent systems are deployed with a fixed capability set. They may learn from memory or improve prompts, but their search space remains bounded by the original architecture.

Stage 29 introduces open-ended intelligence as a controlled mode of continuous capability search.

This article closes the world-contact and open-ended cognition arc. It follows [Stage 28: Developmental Cognition](article-29-development.md), where capability maturation is gated by readiness, and prepares the operational arc that begins with [Stage 30: Operational Primitives](article-31-operational-intelligence.md).

## Self-evolution integration

Open-ended mode integrates reflection, development, abstraction, curiosity, and constitutional stability. The system can propose new strategies, workflows, agent roles, or architectural mutations when evidence suggests a persistent limitation.

These proposals remain subject to review and constraints. Open-endedness is not permission for unbounded mutation.

## Capability search

The system searches for capabilities that improve prediction, task success, robustness, or transfer. Search can arise from repeated failure, curiosity pressure, contradiction, or developmental readiness.

The search target is structural improvement, not merely a better answer to one prompt.

## Constitutional constraints

Every proposed mutation is checked against the constitutional stability layer. Unsafe drift, reward corruption, and epistemic degradation must be blocked or quarantined.

This makes open-ended intelligence a constrained process rather than a runaway one.

## Emergent behaviour analysis

Long-horizon behaviour must be analyzed as it unfolds. The system records capability proposals, accepted changes, rejected changes, outcomes, and side effects.

This evidence allows the architecture to study its own evolution and to distinguish beneficial emergence from destabilizing drift.
